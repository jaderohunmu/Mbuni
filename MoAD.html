Museum Of The African Diaspora Redesigned

I collaborated with a creative team to reinvent a new and exciting MoAD website redesign. This project involves everything from photography, web design, and online events.

Overview

With museum closures around the country due to COVID-19, MoAD has vastly increased in online programming and wanted to develop a new and exciting digital voice connecting the Museum to virtual audiences worldwide.

We Uncovered accessibility issues within existing website, reduced time user took to both find live events and become a member. Redesigned the website to help compete with other contemporary museums using black background that helped strengthen feature exhibitions display

The Problem...is COVID


What a time to be alive! With museum closures around the country due to COVID-19, MoAD has vastly increased in online programming and wanted to develop a new and exciting digital voice connecting the Museum to virtual audiences worldwide. As a team of eager designers (and frequent patron of the Museum), we wanted to assist MoAD in its quest to reinvigorate and excite.

How might we make the museum experience more informative and interactive for the users, especially during the time of COVID?

Stakeholder Brief


To being our redesign journey, we first reached out to the MoADs Digital Director to better understand the business objectives MoAD had set for their website. Mark had envisioned three core objectives that would define the future of the MoADs digital experience:


Previous Next

1) Artist can share their ideas and work similar via blogs/online magazines,

2) Artist and Visitors can reach out and communicate with each other,

3) Visitors can purchase art from artist


MoAD had already engaged with an agency to help redesign their website. However, that didn't deter us from learning more about Mark's vision and what MoAD wanted to accomplish given the 270% increase in online event participation during the pandemic.





Reinventing MoAD's Website


With an idea of what the business wants. We set out on a journey to understand what MoAD's supporting community needs. First, we started to develop proto-personas and research questions about the MoADs community and how they experience art culture during the pandemic
